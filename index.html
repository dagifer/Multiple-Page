One of my best Venezuelan dishes is Arepa. It is a typical dish for breakfast also you can eat on dinner.

Arepa are from general between Colombia and Venezuela but the different is that Colombian arepa are thinner and are not filled with anything, while Venezuelan arepa are filled and thicker.

Arepa made of ground corn flour, water and salt. You need to put water and pinch of salt in a bowl after you pour cornflour and mixed all them with your fingers until the consistency is solid. Then you create a little flat circle with your hands, like if use your hands to clap and put it on a grill or a frypan until you see a golden or brown approximate 5 minutes per side. After you cut it in the middle and filled with whatever you choose.

The most traditional in the Venezuelan arepa is filling because each of them has a name. I will do my best translate below:

  • Shredded beef and cheddar cheese. We call "Pelua" which means is "furry"
  • Black bean and cheese. "Domino" which means is "Dominoes"
  • Chicken and avocado. Reina Pepiada which means is "The queen"
